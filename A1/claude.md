High-dimensional biomedical data offers the potential to uncover biological and medical insights for complex diseases. Your collaborator Harry is a nephrologist (kidney specialist) who is interested in detecting graft rejection in kidney transplant patients. He wants to know whether blood-based samples or biopsy-based samples lead to better performance.

For this task, Harry wants to use GSE46474 for the blood-based samples and GSE138043 for the biopsy-based samples.
Part 1 - Exploratory Analysis
Max 200 words, max 2 figures

Harry wants to know which genes are the most differentially expressed between patients with graft rejection versus patients with stable grafts. In each of the two data-sets (blood-based and biopsy-based), list the gene symbols of the 20 most differentially expressed genes, and comment on the similarities or differences between the two lists. Ensure that you describe any pre-processing steps and give complete justification for your claims, and any decisions you make in the analytical workflow.
Part 2 - Visualisation
Max 200 words, max 2 figures

Harry wants to visualise all the features to graphically identify the potentially important genes that are different between patients with graft rejection versus patients with stable grafts. For each of the blood-based and biopsy-based data sets, create visualisation(s) that allow Harry to identify the most relevant genes. Justify your choice of visualisation(s), and comment on the similiarity or differences between the two data sets.
Part 3 - Model Building and Evaluation
Max 400 words, max 4 figures

Harry wants to build a classification model using 20 genes that predicts whether a patient is experiencing graft rejection or has a stable graft. Build separate models from the blood-based and biopsy-based data sets. What is the best performance that Harry could reasonably expect when deploying a model for new patients? Comment on the similiarity or differences in the performance between the blood-based and biopsy-based models. Ensure that you give complete justification of your claims and any decisions you make in the analytical workflow.
